Interesting facts about Pigeons!

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.

  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.

  • Pigeons They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to.

Interesting facts about Pandass!

  • Panda tails, which are between 4–6 in (10–15 cm) in length, are the second longest of all species in the bear family.

  • Pandas have broad paws with furry undersides and long retractile claws that help them grip when climbing trees.

  • Giant pandas are omnivores, which means their diet consists of both vegetation and meat. However, bamboo is by far their favourite food.

Interesting facts about Teddy Bears!

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.

  • "Hug A Bear Day" is celebrated annually, especially by teddy bear manufacturers and toy shops, on November 7.

  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

Interesting facts about Camels!

  • Camels ears are small and covered in hair to prevent sand and dust from getting in.

  • Thick lips of camels allow them to eat prickly plants easily without hurting themselves.

  • Baby Camels are born without humps. They are however able to run within hours of birth.

Interesting facts about Chameleons!

  • Each eye of a Chameleon has the incredible range of motion of 90 degrees vertically and 180 degrees horizontally.

  • After a chameleon gets both eyes locked onto its prey, the reptile’s ultra-sticky tongue, can be deployed and reeled back in in less than a second.

  • Skin crystals in Chameleons allow them to change their color according to their will.

Interesting facts about Cicadas!

  • Cicadas have overall 5 eyes of wich two obvious compound eyes and three ocelli, which are believed to detect light and darkness

  • Cicadas are mostly beneficial. They prune mature trees, aerate the soil, and once they die, their bodies serve as an important source of nitrogen for growing trees.

  • Most Cicadas have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
